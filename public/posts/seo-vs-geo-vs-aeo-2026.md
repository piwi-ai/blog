# Search Everywhere Optimization: SEO vs GEO vs AEO in 2026

**7 min read**

## Quick Answer

**SEO (Search Engine Optimization)** gets you found on Google when people search for solutions.

**GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)** gets you recommended by AI chatbots like ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity.

**AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)** gets you featured in instant answers, voice search, and AI Overviews - before anyone even clicks.

In 2026, you need all three. Together, they form one complete strategy: **Search Everywhere Optimization.**

## What is Search Everywhere Optimization?

For 20 years, "getting found online" meant one thing: ranking on Google.

Not anymore.

People now search in three completely different ways:
- They **Google** a topic and scroll through results
- They **ask AI** (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity) for a direct recommendation
- They get an **instant answer** from voice assistants, AI Overviews, or featured snippets - without clicking anything

If you're only optimizing for Google, you're invisible in the other two.

**Search Everywhere Optimization** is the practice of making sure your business shows up no matter how people search. It combines SEO, GEO, and AEO into one unified strategy.

---

## What is SEO?

SEO (Search Engine Optimization) is the practice of making your website rank higher on Google and Bing.

When someone types "invoice automation software" into Google, the algorithm decides which websites to show. SEO is about making sure yours is one of them.

### Core SEO Strategies:

- **Keywords:** Use the exact phrases people search for
- **Backlinks:** Get credible websites to link to yours
- **Content:** Write detailed, authoritative guides (1500+ words)
- **Technical:** Fast loading, mobile-friendly, clean URL structure

**Is SEO still relevant?** Absolutely. Google still processes over 8 billion searches per day. SEO isn't dead - it's just not enough on its own anymore.

---

## What is GEO?

GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) is the practice of making sure AI models know about you, understand what you do, and recommend you when people ask for advice.

Here's the shift:

- **SEO:** "Show me a list of options"
- **GEO:** "Tell me what to use"

When someone asks ChatGPT "What's the best tool for document automation?", the AI doesn't search Google. It gives a direct answer based on what it already knows. If your business isn't clearly represented in its training data, you don't get recommended.

### GEO Strategies You Can Implement Today:

- **Be specific:** "We automate document processing using AI" beats "We leverage technology to transform workflows"
- **Use real numbers:** "98% accuracy, 75% time reduction" is citation-worthy. Vague claims aren't.
- **Get mentioned:** Reviews on G2, Capterra, and media mentions all feed into AI training data
- **Publish case studies:** AI models cite sources with verifiable, real-world results
- **Structured FAQs:** Clear question-answer format is easy for AI to extract and reference

**The test:** Ask ChatGPT, Claude, or Perplexity about your industry right now. Does your business get mentioned? If not, you have a GEO problem.

---

## What is AEO?

AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) is the practice of getting your content featured as the direct answer - before anyone clicks.

You've seen this in action:
- **Google AI Overviews:** The AI-generated summary at the top of search results
- **Featured Snippets:** The boxed answer above traditional results
- **Voice Assistants:** When Alexa or Siri reads an answer out loud
- **"People Also Ask" boxes:** Expandable Q&A sections

**Why does this matter?** Over 50% of Google searches now end without a single click. People get the answer directly from the results page. If you're not in that answer, you've already lost.

![Google AI Overview showing best intelligent document processing solutions](/images/google-ai-overview-idp.png)
*This is what AEO looks like. Google's AI Overview answers the question directly before anyone clicks. Zero clicks required.*

### Common AEO Strategies:

- **Answer questions directly:** Put the answer in the first 2-3 sentences, not buried at the bottom
- **Use FAQ format:** Structure content as clear Q&A with proper headings
- **Schema markup:** Add [structured data](https://schema.org/) code so search engines understand your content
- **Short paragraphs:** Keep paragraphs under 3-4 sentences for easy extraction
- **Target question-based keywords:** "What is," "How to," "Why does" queries

## SEO vs GEO vs AEO: How Do They Compare?

| Aspect | SEO | GEO | AEO |
|--------|-----|-----|-----|
| **Target** | Google, Bing | ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity | AI Overviews, Voice Search, Snippets |
| **User Intent** | "Show me options" | "Tell me what to use" | "Give me a quick answer" |
| **Success Metric** | Page rank, traffic | AI citations | Featured snippet, voice results |
| **Content Style** | Keyword-optimized | Clear, factual, definitive | Concise, structured, Q&A format |
| **Click Required?** | Yes | Sometimes | No (zero-click) |

**The overlap:** All three reward clear, structured, credible content. Write well for one, and you're helping all three.

## How to Do Search Everywhere Optimization

You don't need three separate strategies. Focus on these five principles and they work for all three simultaneously.

### 1. Be Unmistakably Clear About What You Do

Your homepage should explain what you do in one sentence that a 10-year-old could understand.

- ❌ "We leverage intelligent technology to transform enterprise workflows"
- ✅ "We automate document processing using AI. Upload an ID or contract, and we extract and fill your forms automatically."

Clear language = better Google ranking + better AI recommendations + better featured snippets.

### 2. Use Real Numbers

Specific data is the currency of Search Everywhere Optimization.

- ❌ "We save businesses time and money"
- ✅ "We reduce document processing time by 75% with 98% data extraction accuracy"

Real numbers get cited by AI, extracted as featured snippets, and ranked higher by Google.

### 3. Publish Case Studies

A case study with a real problem, real solution, and real results works for:
- **SEO:** Long-form content Google can index
- **GEO:** Verifiable proof AI can cite
- **AEO:** Specific results that can appear as featured answers

Format: Problem → Solution → Specific outcome (time saved, money saved, errors eliminated)

### 4. Get Mentioned on Credible Platforms

Every credible mention is a signal across all three channels:
- **[G2](https://g2.com), [Capterra](https://capterra.com):** Reviews that AI models reference
- **Industry publications:** Backlinks for SEO + training data for GEO
- **[Product Hunt](https://producthunt.com):** High-authority domain that feeds into all three
- **Podcasts:** Transcripts get indexed by Google and inform AI models

### 5. Structure Everything

Use clear headings, bullet points, tables, and FAQ sections. This makes your content:
- Easy to rank for featured snippets (AEO)
- Easy for AI to extract and reference (GEO)
- Easy for Google to understand and rank (SEO)

## FAQs

**Q: Will GEO and AEO replace SEO?**
No. All three work together. SEO drives traditional search traffic. GEO drives AI recommendations. AEO captures zero-click answers. You need all three.

**Q: Where do I start if I have zero presence?**
Start with your website. Make it crystal clear what you do, who you serve, and what results you deliver. Everything else builds on that foundation.

**Q: How do I know if my GEO is working?**
Ask ChatGPT, Claude, or Perplexity about your industry and see if you get mentioned. Test monthly and track progress.

**Q: Does AEO require technical skills?**
The basics don't. Write clear Q&A content with proper headings. For schema markup, most CMS platforms have plugins that handle this automatically.

**Q: How often should I update content for GEO?**
Every 3-6 months. AI models retrain regularly, so fresh, updated content stays relevant longer.
